Design Questionnaire
§Project

Tell us about your home.

This questionnaire helps us understand your vision, your daily life, and the home you've been imagining. There are no wrong answers. The more honest and specific you are, the better we can design for you. If a question doesn't apply, skip it. The box at the top is for everything we forgot to ask.

§0 · Opening

Anything you want us to know.

Before any specifics: tell us a story, a feeling, a memory of a place, a constraint, a worry, a half-formed idea. Whatever brought you here. The questions below are a guide, not a script — this box is yours to use however you'd like.

§1 · Contact

Who we'll be working with.

Street address if known, otherwise neighborhood, city, or general area.
If someone sent you our way, we'd love to know who to thank.
§2 · You

You and your life.

Help us picture how the house will actually be lived in.

Names, ages, relationships. Pets too.
Mornings, evenings, weekends. Where do people gather? Which rooms get used? Which are ignored?
§3 · Project

The project itself.

A range is fine. Note whether you mean conditioned space only, or include garage.
Already chosen? A few in mind? Need recommendations? Tell us.
If yes, tell us what worked, what didn't, and what you're hoping to do differently. Honesty here helps a lot.
§4 · Character

Design character.

We care more about how a home feels than what category it falls into.

Timeless Traditional
Classical proportion, restraint, permanence. Built to age gracefully.
New Old
Classical with a contemporary sensibility — warmth and craft with a modern eye.
Shingle Style
Asymmetry, complex rooflines, natural materials, American vernacular charm.
European Farmhouse
Plaster, aged stone, simple massing with a rural European sensibility.
Arts & Crafts
Handmade quality, honest materials, low eaves, human scale.
Modern Transitional
Clean lines, open plan, but warm — not cold or industrial.
Coastal / Lowcountry
Porches, natural ventilation, raised structures, easy living.
I'll know it when I see it
I have images and references that communicate it better than words.
"Warm." "Lived-in." "Serious but not stiff." Whatever feels true.
Specific houses, architects, neighborhoods, hotels. Pinterest links welcome.
Just as useful as what you love.
§5 · Spaces

Spaces and program.

Pick two or three. Tell us the size, adjacencies, character, what happens there.
Dog wash, gun safe, wine collection, library shelving, specific closets.
§6 · Site

Your site.

The site is as much a part of the design as the house itself.

A view, a tree, the morning light, the grade, the street presence.
Setbacks, easements, FAR limits, HOA, flood zone, steep grade, neighbors close, road noise.
Where's the driveway? Family entrance vs. front door? What outdoor spaces matter, and how do they connect to the inside?
§7 · Budget

Budget & timeline.

We believe in transparency about money. The more honest you can be, the better we can serve you.

Construction only — not land, fees, furnishings, or landscape. A range is fine.
A move-in target tells us a lot more than a construction-start date.
§8 · Last

Last thoughts.

Budget, timeline, communication, scope creep, the unknowns of construction.
Not the style — the feeling. What do you want guests to feel walking in? What do you want to feel at the end of a long day?
